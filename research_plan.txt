# Research Steps

## Step 1

### Review previous ways in which EBOD has been conducted in any country related to climate change

Tasks:

* Study existing EBOD methodologies
* Review country-wise case studies
* Understand frameworks used for climate-related burden estimation
* Identify commonly used indicators, equations, and assumptions

Expected Output:

* Summary table of EBOD methodologies
* Comparative notes across countries

---

## Step 2

### Review literature to understand what health outcomes are associated with climate change

Tasks:

* Identify climate-sensitive diseases and health outcomes
* Review peer-reviewed literature
* Categorize outcomes:

  * Heat-related
  * Air pollution-related
  * Vector-borne
  * Respiratory
  * Cardiovascular
  * Mental health, etc.

Expected Output:

* Disease/outcome literature review sheet
* Categorized health outcome list

---

## Step 3

### Identify evidence relevant for the Indian context (relative risk, exposure-response, etc.)

Tasks:

* Find India-specific studies
* Collect:

  * Relative Risk (RR)
  * Exposure-response relationships
  * Confidence intervals
  * TMREL values
  * Environmental exposure data
* Identify usable datasets and missing variables

Expected Output:

* India-specific evidence table
* Data source mapping sheet

## Step 4

### Review literature and identify the appropriate model to associate environmental burden, climate change, and listed health outcomes along with required data inputs

Tasks:

* Identify suitable modeling approach
* Understand relationships between:

  * Climate variables
  * Environmental exposure
  * Health outcomes
* Finalize:

  * Model structure
  * Equations
  * Required inputs
  * Assumptions
* Review burden calculation methods

Expected Output:

* Finalized methodological framework
* Model pipeline and data requirement sheet

Tasks:

* Generate tables and graphs
* Summarize findings in simple format
* Prepare clear interpretation of results

Expected Output:
