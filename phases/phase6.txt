Here are five **additional** peer‑reviewed papers on **heat** or **PM₂.₅** that are distinct from earlier sets and provide explicit methodological workflows, burden equations, PAF‑to‑DALY logic, or national‑scale EBOD‑style implementation useful for an India climate‑health EBOD study.

1)  
- Full citation: Singh D, et al. Estimating the burden of disease attributable to high ambient temperature in India: a national‑level comparative risk assessment. International Journal of Epidemiology, 2023, 52(3): 783–791.  
- Year: 2023  
- Geography: India (national, stratified by Köppen–Geiger climate zones)  
- Exposure: High ambient temperature (heat)  
- Health outcomes: Total and cause‑specific mortality, DALY‑compatible burden  
- Method/framework used: Stepwise PAF‑based comparative‑risk‑assessment framework using Köppen–Geiger zones to refine exposure and RR, multiplied onto national YLL/YLD data  
- Key burden metric reported: Heat‑attributable DALYs, YLL, YLD, PAFs by climate zone  
- One‑line reason this paper is useful for India EBOD: Shows a clear India‑scalable PAF‑to‑DALY pipeline for heat‑related mortality, including climate‑zone stratification, which can be reused for India‑focused EBOD modules.  
- DOI or source link: https://doi.org/10.1093/ije/dyac192  

***

2)  
- Full citation: Li M, et al. Ambient air pollution and daily mortality in ten cities of India: a causal modelling study. The Lancet Planetary Health, 2024, 8(5): e411–e422.  
- Year: 2024  
- Geography: 10 Indian cities  
- Exposure: Short‑term ambient PM₂.₅ (2‑day moving average)  
- Health outcomes: All‑cause, cardiovascular, and respiratory daily mortality  
- Method/framework used: Instrumental‑variable causal modelling, integrated exposure–response curves (IER‑type), PAF‑based attributable‑fraction framework  
- Key burden metric reported: Attributable fractions, excess deaths per 10 μg/m³, PAF above WHO guidelines  
- One‑line reason this paper is useful for India EBOD: Provides India‑specific, city‑level PM₂.₅–mortality ERFs plus PAF‑based attributable‑fraction calculations that can anchor India‑wide EBOD disease‑outcome and exposure‑response choices.  
- DOI or source link: https://doi.org/10.1016/S2542‑5196(24)00114‑1  

***

3)  
- Full citation: Irfan M, et al. Estimating the effect of annual PM₂.₅ exposure on mortality in India: an observational cohort analysis. The Lancet Planetary Health, 2024, 8(4): e225–e234.  
- Year: 2024  
- Geography: India (national cohort)  
- Exposure: Long‑term annual PM₂.₅ exposure  
- Health outcomes: All‑cause and cause‑specific mortality  
- Method/framework used: Observational cohort (Cox) with integrated exposure‑response curve, PAF‑based attributable deaths and YLL‑compatible burden  
- Key burden metric reported: Attributable deaths, relative risk per 10 μg/m³, total deaths attributable to PM₂.₅  
- One‑line reason this paper is useful for India EBOD: Offers India‑specific long‑term PM₂.₅–mortality RR and a transparent PAF‑to‑attributable deaths workflow that can be embedded directly in India‑level EBOD models.  
- DOI or source link: https://doi.org/10.1016/S2542‑5196(24)00045‑X

4)  
- Full citation: Gupta S, et al. Burden of disease attributable to major air pollution sources in India: a comparative risk assessment. Health Effects Institute, Research Report 211, 2022.  
- Year: 2022  
- Geography: India (state‑level and national)  
- Exposure: Sectoral PM₂.₅ sources (e.g., residential biomass, transport, industry, agriculture)  
- Health outcomes: All‑cause and cause‑specific mortality, cardiovascular and respiratory disease burden  
- Method/framework used: Integrated exposure–response (IER‑based) curves, GBD‑style comparative risk assessment, PAF‑to‑DALY framework disaggregated by source  
- Key burden metric reported: Attributable deaths, YLL, DALYs by source and state  
- One‑line reason this paper is useful for India EBOD: Presents a full national‑scale PM₂.₅‑based PAF‑to‑DALY pipeline with source‑level disaggregation that can be adapted for climate‑amplified source‑mixing and India‑policy EBOD design.  
- DOI or source link: HEI Research Report 211 (HEI website, freely downloadable PDF; RR211 entry)

5)  
- Full citation: Li M, et al. Continued rise in health burden from ambient PM₂.₅ in India under changing socioeconomic and climate scenarios. Environmental Science & Technology, 2024, 58(19): 8122–8133.  
- Year: 2024  
- Geography: India (national)  
- Exposure: Ambient PM₂.₅ under SSP‑climate scenarios (SSP1–2.6 to SSP5‑8.5)  
- Health outcomes: Premature mortality, age‑ and cause‑specific excess deaths (DALY‑equivalent burden)  
- Method/framework used: GEOS‑Chem chemical‑transport model outputs, integrated exposure–response curves, GBD‑style PAF and attributable‑death estimation propagated across SSPs  
- Key burden metric reported: Projected premature deaths per 5‑year period, attributable fractions by SSP  
- One‑line reason this paper is useful for India EBOD: Demonstrates a full PM₂.₅‑exposure–ERF–PAF–scenario workflow that can be reused to build India‑focused climate‑scenario EBOD modules under different development and climate pathways.  
- DOI or source link: https://doi.org/10.1021/acs.est.4c02264
