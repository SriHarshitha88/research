Droughts and climate‑driven rainfall shocks are consistently linked to undernutrition in India and South Asia, with India‑specific studies showing higher undernutrition among drought‑exposed women and children, global and India‑focused reviews describing the pathways from climate extremes to food insecurity and malnutrition, and several quantitative RRs/ORs and burden estimates that can feed into EBOD Step 3.  Below is a 13‑item evidence table for the **Undernutrition and drought** cluster, followed by the top 5 to prioritize and key Indian evidence gaps. [southasia.ifpri](http://southasia.ifpri.info/files/2024/12/1.-Saif_Nihal_Oral.pdf)

## Top 5 papers to prioritize first (1‑line reason each)

1. **Assessing the impact of drought on women and child undernutrition in India (IFPRI, 2024)** – India district‑level panel with explicit ORs/RRs for drought‑linked undernutrition, directly usable for climate‑related nutrition risk in EBOD. [southasia.ifpri](http://southasia.ifpri.info/files/2024/12/1.-Saif_Nihal_Oral.pdf)
2. **Coping through a drought: the association between child nutritional status and drought exposure (2020)** – Multi‑country but India‑relevant ORs for drought→child undernutrition; can be used to benchmark and calibrate India‑specific RRs. [pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10195421/)
3. **Effects of climate change on food security and nutrition in India (2025)** – India‑specific, scenario‑based analysis of drought‑linked food‑security and undernutrition trends; ideal for EBOD counterfactuals and future scenarios. [sciencedirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S266604902500009X)
4. **The impact of drought on the health and livelihoods of women and children (2021)** – India‑focused review that synthesizes drought–undernutrition mechanisms and selected ORs, useful for contextualizing and justifying RRs in EBOD. [sciencedirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0190740920323318)
5. **WHO India nutrition profile (2019)** – Provides baseline undernutrition prevalence and burden by state; when combined with drought‑related ORs, enables estimation of climate‑attributable undernutrition in India. [who](https://www.who.int/india/health-topics/nutrition)

## 3 biggest evidence gaps for this cluster in India

1. **India‑specific RR/HR estimates for undernutrition by drought severity and frequency (per SDI, per drought month, per SPI unit).**  
   Existing India‑specific studies largely report elevated odds in drought‑prone regions but rarely give explicit RRs tied to continuous drought metrics (e.g., per standard deviation in SPI or per drought year), which limits dose‑response modelling in EBOD. [pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10195421/)

2. **High‑resolution, state‑ or district‑level link between drought exposure and specific undernutrition indicators (stunting, wasting, underweight) over time.**  
   NFHS‑5 and other surveys provide rich baseline prevalence, but India‑wide, annually resolved datasets that jointly model drought exposure and detailed undernutrition trajectories at subnational level are still limited. [assets.publishing.service.gov](https://assets.publishing.service.gov.uk/media/57a08ac540f0b652dd0008cc/CPRC-IIPA44.pdf)

3. **Integrated assessment of drought–food‑price–social‑protection pathways on undernutrition.**  
   Many India‑focused studies emphasize drought and agriculture; there is less work that jointly quantifies how droughts translate into food‑price spikes, social‑safety‑net failure, and child undernutrition, constraining policy‑scenario EBOD for nutrition‑specific interventions. [preventionweb](https://www.preventionweb.net/news/india-making-children-resilient-droughts)
