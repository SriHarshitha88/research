## Top 5 papers to prioritize first

1. **Excess Mortality Risk Due to Heat Stress in Different Climatic Zones of India (Environ Sci Technol, 2024)** – India multi‑city time‑series with explicit % excess mortality, CIs, and lag by heat‑stress category; ideal for short‑term heat–mortality functions by zone. [pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/38151765/)
2. **Heat‑related mortality in India: excess all‑cause mortality associated with the 2010 Ahmedabad heat wave (PLOS One, 2014)** – Provides clear event‑level excess deaths and RRs for an extreme heatwave, directly usable for scenario‑based EBOD calculations. [journals.plos](https://journals.plos.org/plosone/article/file?type=printable&id=10.1371%2Fjournal.pone.0091831)
3. **Abnormal temperatures and mortality in India, 2000–2019 (Lancet Planetary Health)** – National‑scale annual heat‑attributable mortality (≈83,700 deaths/year) gives a strong India burden anchor for Step 3. [science.thewire](https://science.thewire.in/health/over-7-lakh-deaths-a-year-in-india-linked-to-abnormal-temperatures-study/)
4. **Ambient temperature and cardiovascular mortality meta‑analysis (PeerJ, 2017)** – Supplies CVD‑specific RRs per heat increment for use when you need cardiovascular‑focused heat‑mortality curves. [peerj](https://peerj.com/articles/3574/)
5. **Global abnormal‑temperature mortality study (Lancet Planetary Health, 5 million deaths/year)** – Offers a consistent global framework and region‑specific AFs and deaths, useful to cross‑check India‑specific numbers and for sensitivity ranges. [facebook](https://www.facebook.com/groups/420911468613467/posts/1827492961288637/)

## Biggest evidence gaps for India in this cluster

1. **Cause‑specific (cardiovascular, respiratory, renal) heat‑mortality RRs from Indian datasets.**  
   Most India studies (Ahmedabad, multi‑city EST paper) report all‑cause mortality, not disaggregated CVD, respiratory, or renal deaths, forcing EBOD to rely on global meta‑analyses for cause‑specific heat RRs. [escardio](https://www.escardio.org/communities/councils/cardiology-practice/scientific-documents-and-publications/ejournal/volume-22/climate-change-and-cardiovascular-disease-the-impact-of-heat-and-heat-health-a/)

2. **Nationwide, long‑term time‑series and cohorts linking daily heat exposure to mortality across diverse Indian climates.**  
   Existing work covers selected cities or specific events; there is no large national time‑series or cohort capturing daily temperature–mortality relationships across rural districts, semi‑arid vs coastal zones, and different socio‑economic settings. [ceew](https://www.ceew.in/publications/mapping-climate-risks-and-impacts-of-extreme-heatwave-disaster-in-indian-districts)

3. **Robust, validated estimates of under‑reported heat deaths and standardized heat‑mortality attribution methods.**  
   Reported heat‑wave deaths likely underestimate true mortality; newer district‑wise excess‑death analyses suggest far higher burdens but use differing definitions and methods, limiting comparability and incorporation into official EBOD. [heathealth](https://heathealth.info/news/lost-in-the-heat-the-critical-miscalculation-in-indias-heatwave-mortality-data/)
